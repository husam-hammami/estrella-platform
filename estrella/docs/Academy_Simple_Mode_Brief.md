# Estrella Academy Simple Mode Brief

This supersedes the heavy dossier/workspace approach for lesson screens.

The new direction is:

**Treat the learner like a bright, distracted beginner who needs the idea to feel safe, obvious, and instantly doable.**

This does not mean childish. It means:

- no intimidation;
- no corporate jargon;
- no serious training-room energy;
- no text walls;
- no complex workspaces;
- no "figure it out" screens;
- no five things competing for attention.

The Academy should feel like a premium, playful micro-training app: **Apple Health clarity, Estrella gold, one tiny career win at a time.**

## Core Rule

One screen. One idea. One choice. One tiny win.

If a learner cannot understand the screen in three seconds, the screen is wrong.

## New Lesson Loop

Every lesson must follow this simple loop:

1. **See It**
   - One simple idea.
   - One short sentence.
   - Optional small visual.

2. **Pick It**
   - Two or three choices.
   - Big tappable cards.
   - No intimidating language.

3. **Get It**
   - One short feedback line.
   - No lecture.
   - No grading tone.

4. **Keep It**
   - A gold check, ring, sticker, chip, or small artifact piece appears.
   - The learner sees progress immediately.

5. **Next**
   - One clear next action.

## Hard Limits

These limits are mandatory:

- Max one main idea per screen.
- Max 30 words of explanation before interaction.
- Max 3 choices on a normal lesson screen.
- Max 1 Estrella sentence visible at a time.
- No permanent chat column.
- No three-column lesson layout.
- No long method text.
- No visible course pedagogy terms.
- No "module", "rubric", "framework", "artifact piece", "dossier", "operating model", or corporate labels in learner UI.
- No serious academic tone.
- No parchment-heavy workspace styling.

## Visual Direction

Apple Health, but gold.

Use:

- bright warm white background;
- soft cream secondary surfaces;
- gold rings and progress fills;
- large simple cards;
- friendly rounded controls;
- small premium icons;
- clear progress circles;
- gentle success animation;
- plenty of breathing room;
- one obvious button.

Avoid:

- old book layouts inside lessons;
- dense desk/workspace metaphors;
- beige parchment overload;
- tiny labels;
- long sidebars;
- dashboards;
- tables;
- dramatic gamification;
- childish cartoons.

## Asset Rule

Use the existing generated/course assets as the visual identity for each course.

Do not replace them with generic flat icons, abstract circles, placeholder illustrations, or newly invented cheap objects.

The asset should appear as a premium visual element in the background corner or hero area:

- subtle;
- dimensional when possible;
- partially tucked behind the lesson card or folder surface;
- large enough to give the course identity;
- quiet enough that the learner still focuses on the one simple action.

The asset is not the whole UI. It is the course's visual anchor.

## Tone

The voice should sound like a kind human explaining something to a smart young learner.

Use:

- short words;
- direct sentences;
- plain examples;
- "pick one";
- "try this";
- "save this";
- "you got it";
- "next";
- "this means..."

Avoid:

- "leverage";
- "strategic";
- "framework";
- "methodology";
- "stakeholder";
- "artifact";
- "competency";
- "rubric";
- "executive";
- "optimize";
- "synergy";
- "transformation".

## Estrella Coach

Estrella is a tiny hint, not a chat panel.

Default state:

- collapsed small gold circle or small note.

When visible:

- one sentence only;
- no more than 16 words;
- calm and useful;
- no fake grading;
- no "great job" spam.

Examples:

- "Good. That is the real trigger."
- "Pick the answer you would actually use."
- "Keep the line that feels true."
- "One fact beats ten worries."

## Course Designer Subagent Task

Each course must be redesigned by a specialist subagent before implementation.

The subagent's job is not to write course lectures. The job is to turn the course into a simple sequence of tiny wins.

For each course, output:

1. **Big Promise**
   - One sentence a child could understand.

2. **Progress Toy**
   - The simple visual thing that fills up.
   - Example: ring, path, jar, checklist, map, meter, stack.

3. **Lesson Cards**
   - One card per lesson.
   - Each card must include:
     - tiny idea;
     - simple situation;
     - 2-3 choices;
     - correct/helpful choice behavior;
     - one Estrella line;
     - what fills in progress.

4. **No-Jargon Rewrite**
   - Replace any serious wording with simple human language.

5. **UI Shape**
   - What the learner sees first.
   - What they tap.
   - What changes after they tap.

## Course Simplification Targets

### Confidence Reset

Big promise:

**Learn what makes confidence drop, then pick a small reset move.**

Progress toy:

Gold reset ring with 6 small lights.

Screen style:

One calm card. A simple situation. Pick the real trigger or reset move.

Example lesson:

- Idea: "Confidence can drop for a reason."
- Situation: "You go quiet when someone senior joins."
- Choices:
  - "I felt judged"
  - "The stakes felt high"
  - "I compared myself"
- Estrella: "Pick the one that feels most true."
- Save: gold light fills in the reset ring.

### Personal Branding

Big promise:

**Help people know what to remember you for.**

Progress toy:

Gold profile meter with clear/proof/useful lights.

Screen style:

Show one sentence or profile piece. Pick how to make it clearer.

### Interview Mastery

Big promise:

**Understand the question, then answer calmly.**

Progress toy:

Interview readiness ring.

Screen style:

Show one interview question. Ask: what are they really checking?

### AI For HR Leaders

Big promise:

**Know when AI should help, and when people must decide.**

Progress toy:

Four-zone AI map with gold dots.

Screen style:

Show one HR task. Pick: automate, help, keep human, or not ready.

### Leadership Without Burnout

Big promise:

**Stop carrying everything alone.**

Progress toy:

Energy battery or weekly rhythm ring.

Screen style:

Show one work situation. Pick: keep, hand off, say no, or recover.

### Public Speaking

Big promise:

**Turn nerves into one clear next move.**

Progress toy:

Talk-ready ring.

Screen style:

Show one speaking moment. Pick: open stronger, cut extra, pause, or answer.

### Modern HR Foundations

Big promise:

**See what HR should do next.**

Progress toy:

HR action map with simple lights.

Screen style:

Show one people problem. Pick: process, business, risk, or manager action.

### The Offer Machine

Big promise:

**Know your number and ask clearly.**

Progress toy:

Offer confidence meter.

Screen style:

Show one offer moment. Pick: set floor, set target, ask, or pause.

## Implementation Rule

Do not implement the full Academy first.

First implement one approved Simple Mode vertical slice:

**Confidence Reset lesson screen only.**

It must prove:

- one idea;
- one simple interaction;
- one gold progress change;
- one short Estrella hint;
- no intimidation;
- no serious workspace;
- no text wall.

After that is approved, apply the same simplicity to the other courses.

## Rejection Test

Reject any screen that:

- looks like a course platform;
- has three columns;
- shows more than one main task;
- requires reading a paragraph before doing anything;
- uses jargon;
- feels serious or corporate;
- has a permanent chat panel;
- uses more than three choices;
- makes the learner feel tested instead of guided;
- would overwhelm a distracted beginner.
