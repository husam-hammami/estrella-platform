Why confidence collapses

Confidence is a state, not a trait. It rises and falls with the room, the stakes, and how safe you feel. The same person can be strong in one meeting and small in the next.

That matters because a state can be changed. When you see a low-confidence moment as a state, you stop asking "am I a confident person?" and start asking "what made it drop right there?"

A skilled engineer can give a perfect demo, then go quiet when a senior director walks in. The skill didn't change in those ten seconds. The state did.
