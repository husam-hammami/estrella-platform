Welcome
Dear Brave Soul,
🌸

You picked up this journal for a reason. Maybe you are exhausted. Maybe you feel lost, stuck, or disconnected from the person you know you are capable of being. Maybe something happened that shook your world. Or maybe — and this takes real courage — you simply decided that enough is enough, and you are ready to begin again.

Whatever brought you here, welcome. This is your safe space. These pages hold no judgment, no expectations, and no timelines. They hold only one thing: possibility.

RESET is a 90-day guided journey designed to help you reconnect with yourself, process what you are carrying, and step into the life you truly want. It is not about becoming someone new. It is about remembering who you already are.

"The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. You have already taken it by opening this page."

How to Use This Journal

✦ Daily Pages: Complete your morning and evening check-ins each day. Even five minutes counts.

✦ Weekly Resets: Every 7 days, pause to reflect on your progress and set intentions.

✦ Monthly Reviews: Celebrate your growth and realign your direction each month.

✦ Deep Work Sections: Return to healing, self-discovery, and NLP exercises as needed.

✦ Affirmations: Choose one each morning. Say it out loud. Mean it.

🌱 Remember

There is no "wrong" way to journal. Skip a day? Come back. Write one word? That counts. The only rule is: be honest with yourself. These pages are yours.

Section Two
The Emotional
Wheel
Name it to tame it. Understanding your emotions is the beginning of freedom.
💜

Emotions are not problems to fix — they are messengers. The more precisely you can name what you feel, the more power you have to respond rather than react.

💡 The Science

Research in affective neuroscience shows that naming an emotion — "I feel anxious" vs "I feel bad" — measurably reduces its intensity. This is called affect labelling. The more specific your vocabulary, the calmer your nervous system.

Nobody cares→I am worthy of love and connection

I should have known→I did the best I could with what I knew
